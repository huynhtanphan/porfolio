Cyclistic Behavior

Cyclistic is a bike-sharing service in Chicago with over 5,800 bikes and 600 docking stations. It features special bikes like reclining bikes, hand tricycles, and cargo bikes, catering to people with disabilities and those unable to use standard bikes.

The company aims to maximize the number of annual members, as they are more profitable than casual users. Understanding the different usage patterns between annual members and casual users is crucial.

Methodology:

Using the Six Steps of Data Analysis Process

1. Ask:

- How do annual members and casual users use Cyclistic bikes differently?

- Why would casual users purchase annual memberships?

2. Prepare:

- Collect the most recent 6 months of historical trip data.

- Identify data sources and organization.

3. Process:

- Check for errors and clean data.

- Select tools and methods for data processing.

4. Analyze:

- Organize and format data.

- Perform descriptive analysis like averages, maximum values, and trip counts by day of the week.

- Identify trends and relationships.

5. Share and Act:

- Create reports and data visualizations.

- Provide recommendations based on analysis results.

Charts:
Insights

1. Number of Rides by Rider Type (Casual and Member):

Casual Riders: The number of rides for this group is higher on weekends (Sunday and Saturday). This suggests that casual riders primarily use the bike service for recreational activities or tourism during the weekends.

Member Riders: The number of rides for this group is evenly distributed throughout the week, with peaks on weekdays (Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday). This indicates that members use bikes as a daily mode of transportation, possibly for commuting to work or other daily activities.

2. Average Duration of Rides by Rider Type:

Casual Riders: The average duration of rides for this group is always higher compared to the member group, especially on weekends. This suggests that casual riders tend to use bikes for longer trips, possibly for sightseeing or tourism.

Member Riders: The average duration of rides for this group is relatively low and stable throughout the week. This indicates that members primarily use bikes for shorter trips, possibly for commuting to work or other daily activities.
